How this app works

For tradies. Your books. Your phone. No accountant fees. No subscription. Nothing leaves this device.

1

Snap your receipts

Every time you buy something for work

Bought materials at Travis Perkins? Filled up the van? Got tools from Screwfix?

Tap "🧾 Receipts" → + Add → snap a photo or just type it in. Done.

💡 Tip: do it right there in the shop carpark. 30 seconds. Don't let receipts pile up in your glovebox.

2

Log every job

One job = one piece of work for one customer

New kitchen for Mrs Smith? That's a job. Boiler swap for Mr Patel? Another job.

Tap "🔨 Jobs" → + New → name it, who it's for, what you quoted. Mark it "Done" when finished.

💡 Tip: link receipts to jobs as you go. Then you know if you made or lost money on each one.

3

Send the invoice. Get paid.

Looks proper. Doesn't cost a penny

Job done? Tap "📤 Invoices" → + Draft. Fill in the bits. Tap 📄 Generate PDF.

Print it, save it, email it. When the customer pays, tap ✓ Mark paid.

💡 Tip: chase overdue ones the day they go red. Polite reminder works most of the time.

🚐 Driving for work?

Every mile in the van counts as expense. 45p per mile off your tax bill (first 10,000 miles).

Tap "🚐 Log mileage" on Home. Quick: from → to → miles. Even short trips add up.

💡 28 miles to a job = £12.60 off your tax. Do it every day for a year = £3,000+ saved.

💰 The "set aside for tax" number

Look at the Home page. See the red number top right? That's what HMRC wants when self-assessment is due.

Open a savings account. Move that exact amount every month. Then you're never caught short in January.

💡 Most tradies get nailed because they spent the tax money. This number stops that.

Common questions

Where's my data?

On your phone or laptop. Just here. Not on a server. Not in the cloud. Not even me — the app maker — can see it.

What if my phone breaks?

Tap the ⚙ gear top right → Export backup. Save the file to email, Dropbox, or a USB stick. Restore later on a new phone.

What's CIS?

If you sub-contract for bigger builders, they take 20% (or 30%) off your invoice and pay it to HMRC for you. Set it in Tax → Settings and the app handles the maths.

What's VAT?

Only kicks in if you bill more than £90,000 a year. Most one-person trades don't hit that. If you do, set it in Tax → Settings.

When do I file my tax return?

Online by 31 January each year (for the tax year that ended the previous 5 April). Use Tax → MTD quarterly to get the numbers, then hand them to your accountant or punch them into HMRC's site yourself.

What if I forgot to log something?

Add it now. Set the date to when it actually happened. Better late than never. Don't make stuff up though — only what's real.
