## What it is

An app on your phone. **Not** Xero. **Not** QuickBooks. **Not** TBA a month forever. It's one file that opens in your phone's browser. Lives on your phone. Your data stays on your phone — not on some company's server in Ireland.

It does the stuff you actually do every day:

**Snap a receipt** at Screwfix. Phone reads it. Logs it as an expense. Knows it's tools/materials. Done in five seconds. Tea break length.

**Tap "I drove from Mr Smith's to Mrs Patel's"** — works out your mileage at the HMRC rate (45p first 10k miles, 25p after). Builds up your travel claim automatically.

**Tracks jobs** — "Kitchen refit — Mr Smith." You log materials, hours, miles against it. The app tells you if you actually made money or you got eaten by Travis Perkins markups.

**Makes invoices** — tap a button, it builds a professional invoice from the job. Sends it. Tracks if it got paid or if you need to chase.

**Watches your tax** — running total all year. Tells you "set aside £X this month for the January bill" so you're not staring at HMRC in January with empty pockets.

**Does your MTD** — quarterly submission. Picks the numbers out of everything you've logged. You review, you send.

**Handles CIS properly.** Both sides. "I got paid TBA0 minus TBA CIS" → it records right. "I paid my labourer TBA and need to deduct CIS" → it works out the TBA, tells you what to report. Most apps cock this up. This one doesn't.

## How forking actually works (the bit I know sounds techy but isn't)

You won't need to write code. Promise.

1. I make you a copy of the tool with your name on it. `ivan-builds/my-accounts` or whatever you want.
2. It lives on GitHub Pages — which is just a free webpage host owned by Microsoft.
3. You get a link: `ivan-builds.github.io/my-accounts`.
4. You bookmark it on your phone. Or "Add to Home Screen" so it looks like an app.
5. That's it.

When Dave wants one, I (or you, after you've seen me do it once) copy your version, change "Ivan Builds" to "Dave Plastering," send him his link. He bookmarks it. He's running. He never gave us a credit card, never logged in, never installed an "app."

The whole thing is one HTML file. Smaller than a single photo from your phone. Lives on his phone. Yours on yours. Dave's on Dave's. Nobody can see each other's data. Nobody can sell anyone's data because nobody has anyone's data except themselves.
